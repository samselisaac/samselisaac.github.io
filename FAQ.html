FAQ Procedure on Creating FAQ
Purpose This document provides a standard approach for writing FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions) and Procedures, ensuring clarity, consistency, and usability.
Step 1: Define the Purpose • Ask yourself: What is the problem this FAQ/procedure solves?
• Keep the scope narrow (one document = one main topic).
Step 2: Gather Information • Collect common questions from users, team members, or past issues.
• Identify the key steps for procedures.
• Confirm details with subject matter experts.
Step 3: Write in a Clear Format • Use simple, direct language.
• FAQs: phrase questions in the user’s voice (“How do I…?”).
• Procedures: use numbered steps for actions, bullets for notes.
Step 4: Structure the Document • Title: Clear and descriptive.
• Purpose: One or two sentences about why this document exists.
• FAQs: List questions in logical order, most common first.
• Procedures: Step-by-step instructions, each starting with a verb.
• Tips/Notes: Add cautions, links, or references at the end.
Step 5: Review & Test • Have someone unfamiliar with the process read it.
• Check if they can follow it without extra help.
• Revise for clarity, remove jargon.
Step 6: Maintain & Update • Review regularly (set a schedule: quarterly, annually, etc.).
• Update when processes change or new common questions arise.
• Version/date stamp each document.
Example FAQ Entry Q: How often should I update this FAQ?
A: Review at least once per quarter, or whenever the process changes.
